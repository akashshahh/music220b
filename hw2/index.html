Source Material

All three ideas use recordings of my pitch pipe that I use to help direct my a capella group as the sound source. I recorded a few interactions with the pipe: sustained blowing, tapping, flicking, dropping, and spinning randomly between notes.

Idea 1: Sliding Chords

Layered pitch pipe sound of me blowing into it sort of "improperly" and far away to create a more static-heavy sound. Made slow-moving chords with portamento. Multiple voices of the static sound are pitched to chord tones and glide between chord changes. Added a lot of reverb to create a shoegazy sound.

Idea 2: Layered Intervals

The pitch pipe random sound (spinning between notes) played with a second voice a major third above.

Idea 3: Arpeggio with Drums

Arpeggiated pitch pipe sound over a drum beat I sequenced. The sound is played at different rates to create different pitches. The drums use the tap, flick, and drop sounds.

Milestone 1

Combines the sliding chord pad intro with the arp melody and drums. Opens with the slow-moving chords at half tempo, then transitions into the faster arp section. Doesn't rly have a lot of structure and isn't built out but this milestone for me is more of an exploration of my toolkit with these sounds that I'll later stretch out, explore, and arrange into a full composition.
