Akash Shah

Source Material

All three ideas use recordings of a pitch pipe as the sound source. I recorded various interactions with the pipe: sustained blowing, tapping, flicking, dropping, and spinning randomly between notes.

Idea 1: Sliding Chords

Layered pitch pipe static sound creating slow-moving chords with portamento. Multiple voices of the static sound are pitched to chord tones and glide smoothly between chord changes. Heavy reverb creates a shoegaze-inspired wash of sound.

Idea 2: Layered Intervals

The pitch pipe random sound (spinning between notes) played with a second voice a major third above.

Idea 3: Arpeggio with Drums

Arpeggiated pitch pipe melodies over a drum beat I sequences. The pitch pipe sound is played back at different rates to create pitched notes, forming chord arpeggios. The drums use the tap, flick, and drop sounds as kick, snare, hi-hat.

Milestone 1

Combines the sliding chord pad intro with the arpeggiated melody and drums. The piece opens with slow-moving chords at half tempo, then transitions into the faster arp section.
